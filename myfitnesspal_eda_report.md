# Task 04: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Report

## 1. Engagement Patterns Across Platforms
Our cleaned social signals dataset reveals varying levels of user engagement across major digital communities:
* **YouTube:** Highest overall reach, driven heavily by long-form product comparison and setup video tutorials (e.g., MyFitnessPal vs LoseIt).
* **Discussion Forums (Reddit):** Moderate engagement focusing on targeted community reviews, functional inquiries, and feature requests (such as barcode scanners and app costs).
* **Microblogging (Twitter/X):** High-frequency, immediate-velocity interaction consisting of user streak updates, spontaneous ecosystem reviews, and real-time bug reporting.

## 2. Qualitative Sentiment Analysis
An evaluation of brand and feature mention sentiment highlights distinct themes:
* **Positive:** Centered around long-term lifestyle habit tracking, maintaining daily streak streaks, and community recommendations from active fitness coaches.
* **Negative:** Mostly triggered by pricing complaints concerning premium features following recent updates, alongside occasional API/watch connectivity sync failures.
* **Neutral:** Informational product walkthroughs, feature comparative discussions, and multi-platform tracking setups.

## 3. Preliminary Analytical Observations
* Video content serves as a leading driver for high-volume exposure and software feature validation.
* Premium monetization paywall shifts remain a core driver of immediate critical user backlash across discussion threads.
