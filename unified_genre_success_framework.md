# Task 08: Unified Genre-Level Success Framework

## 1. The Launch Readiness Composite Index (LRCI) Scoring Method
To evaluate any product feature launch across the Health & Fitness genre, we establish a standardized **Launch Readiness Composite Index (LRCI)** scaled from `0 to 100`. 

The scoring formula calculates normalized inputs weighted by generalized genre importance:

$$\text{LRCI} = (0.40 \times \text{Trend Velocity}) + (0.35 \times \text{Engagement Ratio}) + (0.25 \times \text{Influencer Weight})$$

*   **Score > 75:** **Green Light** (High probability of launch success; deploy immediately)
*   **Score 50-75:** **Yellow Light** (Moderate risk; requires targeted feature optimization)
*   **Score < 50:** **Red Light** (High failure risk; discard or re-engineer feature concept)

---

## 2. Cross-Product Feature Importance Benchmarking
While individual apps scale differently, the unified framework weights feature importance across the entire genre according to predictive stability:

1. **Trend Velocity (Weight: 40%):** Universal leading indicator. Measures the literal growth speed of the fitness trend.
2. **Engagement Ratio (Weight: 35%):** Community baseline. Measures if users are active or just silently watching.
3. **Influencer Weight (Weight: 25%):** Growth multiplier. Measures authority pull from creators and athletes.

---

## 3. How to Evaluate New Feature Launches
When a new fitness feature concept is submitted (e.g., a new macro-tracker for MyFitnessPal or a route-sharing tool for Strava):
1. **Collect 7 days of social signals** surrounding the core concept theme.
2. **Normalize the metric values** on a standard 1-100 scale.
3. **Run the LRCI equation** to output a final composite score.
4. **Deploy according to the threshold rules** (Green / Yellow / Red) to eliminate guesswork and protect the core app experience.
